# Vault Run  

## Introduction

Welcome to **Vault Run**, the game where you and your rivals run cash transport companies, chase the best contracts, and try not to overload your truck or run out of money at the worst possible moment. Your goal is simple: make the most money before the game ends, or be the last company still in business.

In **Vault Run**, players move around the board, win transport contracts, and service banks and shops by picking up or delivering cash. Some contracts fill your truck. Others empty it. That means planning your route matters just as much as luck. You will need to decide where to go, what contracts to buy, how much money to keep in your truck, and when to return to Start to rebalance.

## How to win

Before the game starts, all players decide which game mode they want to play.

* **Limited Turns**: The game ends after an agreed number of turns. The player with the most money in their **Cash Vault + truck** wins.
* **Last One Standing**: The game continues until only one player is not bankrupt. That player wins.

## Starting the game

Each player starts with **1,000,000 in the Cash Vault** and an empty truck with room for up to **100,000**.

Before the first turn, each player decides how much money to load from their **Cash Vault** into their truck.

## The Board

![Vault Run Board](vault-run-board.svg)

The board is circular, just like a classic property game. Most regular fields represent places that need cash transport service ("contract fields"). These can be banks, shops, or other businesses. Each contract field has:

- a contract price
- a fixed transport amount
- a type: **pickup** or **deposit**

These values never change during the game.

## Cash Vault, Truck

You have two places where your money can be: Your **Cash Vault** and your **truck**.

* **Your Cash Vault**: This is your safe storage. Money in your **Cash Vault** is not affected by truck capacity.
* **Your truck**: This is the money you are carrying right now. Your truck can carry a maximum of **100,000**. You can never carry more than your truck’s capacity.

You will need both. If your truck is too full, you may not be able to pick up more cash. If your truck is too empty, you may not be able to deliver cash where it is needed.

### The Bank

The **Bank** is the central bank. It is not owned by any player.

The Bank is used whenever money must be paid outside normal player-to-player transactions, such as buying contracts from the game, paying certain fines, or resolving card effects.

### Playing with Money

Whenever a player has to pay money, they may freely choose where that money comes from: The **Cash Vault**, the **truck**, or any combination of both. As long as the total available money is enough, the payment can be made.

## Choosing Your Direction

At the start of **every turn**, **before rolling the die**, you choose which way to drive: **clockwise** or **counterclockwise**. Then you roll a normal **6-sided die** and move your truck that many spaces in the chosen direction. This is one of the key decisions in the game. Choosing the right direction can help you reach good contracts, avoid bad situations, or make it back to Start just in time.

## Start

Whenever you **land on Start or move over it**, you may freely move money between your **Cash Vault** and your **truck**. This is called **rebalancing**. You can unload money from your truck into your **Cash Vault** or load money from your **Cash Vault** into your truck. As always, your truck may never go above its maximum capacity.

Start is your home base. Use it wisely.

## Contract Fields

A player can only work a field if they own the contract for it. If you land on a regular field, one of three things happens.

1. **The field has no owner**: You may buy the contract by paying its contract price to the **Bank**.
2. **You already own the contract**: You must decide whether to fulfill it.
3. **Another player owns the contract**: You may try to buy that contract from the current owner.

## Buying Contracts From Other Players

Negotiation starts at the field’s original contract price multiplied by **1**. The two players then have **30 seconds** to agree on a price. If the seller declines, the buyer may either stop negotiating or raise the offer to the next multiplier. The possible multipliers are:

- **1x**
- **1.5x**
- **2x**
- **3x**
- **5x**
- **10x**

If both players agree on a price within 30 seconds:

- the buyer pays the agreed amount
- the seller receives that money in their **Cash Vault**
- the contract changes owner immediately
- the new owner must decide **within the same turn** whether to fulfill the contract

If no agreement is reached, the contract stays with the current owner.

## Fulfilling contracts

When you land on a field you own, you may fulfill that contract.

* **Pickup field**: You must have enough free space in your truck to take the required amount.
* **Deposit field**: You must already have enough money in your truck to deliver the required amount.

If you fulfill the contract successfully:

- The pickup or delivery happens immediately. The amount is either taken from your truck or added to your truck.
- You earn a service reward equal to **10% of that field’s amount**
- That reward goes straight into your **Cash Vault**

## Failing or Refusing a Contract

If you land on one of your own fields and **cannot** fulfill the contract, or **choose not to**, then:

- you lose the contract immediately
- you must pay a fine equal to **10% of that field’s amount** to the **Bank**

The field becomes unowned again. This means bad planning can cost you twice: you lose the contract and you lose money.

## Chance Fields

If you land on a **Chance** field, draw a Chance card and do what it says. Chance cards can help you, hurt you, move you around the board, cost you money, send you to the Garage, or create other surprises. Some are great. Some are not. That is life on the road.

The Chance deck is shuffled at the start of the game. When a player lands on a **Chance** field, they draw the top card from the deck, resolve its effect immediately, then insert the card back into the deck at a random position.This means every card is always in circulation, no card is ever permanently removed, and you can never be sure when a particular card will come up again.

If a card sends you to another field, you resolve that field immediately in the same round, just as if you had reached it with a normal move.

## Garage

The **Garage** works like the "jail" corner in a classic board game. If you land there normally, you are just visiting. If a rule or card sends you there for maintenance, you must:

- move your truck to the **Garage**
- pay **5,000** to the **Bank**
- do nothing else for the rest of that round

If you are sent there by another effect, the Garage is fully resolved in that same round, just like any other field you are sent to. In short: visiting is harmless, being sent there is not.

## Take a Break

**Take a Break** is the calm corner of the board. Nothing happens there. Enjoy the moment while it lasts. If you are sent there by another effect, you still resolve it in the same round. In this case, that simply means nothing happens.

## Truck Broke - Go to Garage

If you land on this field, your truck breaks down immediately. Move straight to the **Garage**. Do **not** collect or use Start on the way. Once there, you must resolve the **Garage** immediately in the same round:

- pay **5,000** to the **Bank**
- do nothing else in that round

## Bankruptcy

You are bankrupt whenever your **Cash Vault + truck cash** is less than the amount you are required to pay. This can happen when you must pay a fine, a fee, a repair cost, or any other obligation and do not have enough total money available. When paying, you may freely choose whether the money comes from your **Cash Vault**, your **truck**, or a combination of both.

Once you are bankrupt, you are out of the game.

## A few important things to remember

- You choose your direction **before** rolling.
- You may rebalance whenever you **reach or pass Start**.
- Your truck can never carry more than **100,000**.
- Rewards always go into your **Cash Vault**.
- Selling a contract also pays into your **Cash Vault**.
- Buying a contract from the game means paying the **Bank**.
- Failing or refusing your own contract costs you the contract **and** a fine.
- If you take over a contract from another player, you must decide about fulfillment **in that same turn**.
- Contract negotiations against another player last **30 seconds**.
- If you are sent to the Garage for maintenance, you pay **5,000** and your turn is over.
- If you are sent to any field by a card or other effect, you resolve that field in the same round as if you had reached it through a normal move.

## Strategy tips

**Vault Run** is not just about rolling lucky numbers.

To do well, you need to think ahead:

- Should you fill your truck now or keep room for pickups?
- Should you carry lots of cash for deposits, or play it safe?
- Is this contract worth buying?
- Should you go clockwise or counterclockwise this turn?
- Can you afford to risk one more stop before reaching Start?

The best players are the ones who manage their route, their truck, and their contracts better than everyone else.
